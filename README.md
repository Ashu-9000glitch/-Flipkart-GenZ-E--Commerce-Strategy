# Flipkart Gen Z Commerce Strategy

A product strategy case study focused on how Flipkart can better engage Gen Z shoppers through social-led discovery, visual commerce, and circular fashion.

This project explores how Flipkart can evolve from a traditional e-commerce marketplace into a more trend-aware, trust-driven, and visually immersive shopping platform built for Gen Z behavior. 

---

## Overview

Gen Z is now a major force in Indian consumption, influencing spending across fashion, beauty, tech, and lifestyle categories. This strategy case study argues that Flipkart must adapt to a generation that values authenticity, creators, visual discovery, and frictionless mobile-first shopping. 

The project proposes new platform concepts designed to improve discovery, trust, and conversion for Gen Z users. 
---

## Problem Statement

How can Flipkart build a Gen Z commerce experience that feels:
- More social and trend-driven.
- More visual and creator-led.
- More trustworthy for fashion purchases.
- More aligned with mobile-first shopping behavior.
- More relevant to circular and resale-led consumption. 

---

## Key Insights

The research suggests that Gen Z shoppers:
- Discover products through social media and creators, not just search bars.
- Care deeply about fit, styling, and visual confidence before buying.
- Prefer authenticity and tend to reject brands that feel untruthful.
- Are increasingly interested in sustainable and pre-loved fashion.
- Often shop with friends, phones, and creator content influencing the decision.

These behaviors point to a commerce experience that needs to feel closer to culture and less like a traditional catalog.

## Proposed Concepts

### Creator HQ
Creator HQ bridges the gap between seeing a trend on social media and buying it with confidence on Flipkart. It turns influencers into trusted discovery channels with branded mini-storefronts and seamless checkout. 

### Vibe Lens
Vibe Lens goes beyond conventional visual search by recognizing style cues like silhouette, wash, and aesthetic intent. This helps users find products that match the look they want, not just the color they typed.

### Spoyl Thrift
Spoyl Thrift addresses the growing demand for sustainable and affordable circular fashion. It creates an organized marketplace for resale and pre-loved apparel inside the Flipkart ecosystem.

## User Behavior

The case study identifies a few important Gen Z shopping patterns:
- Shopping is social and often collaborative.
- Visual immersion matters more than static product photography.
- Creators are becoming search engines.
- Mobile phones influence both online and in-store behavior.
- Brand authenticity directly affects trust and loyalty.

These insights support the need for a more immersive, trust-first commerce design.

---

## Metrics for Success

The success of this strategy can be measured through:
- Click-through rate on visual recommendations.
- Time to checkout.
- Churn rate.
- Net Promoter Score.
- My Twin participation rate.
- Return-to-origin reduction.
- Repeat purchase frequency.
- Circularity participation. 

These metrics cover both short-term adoption and long-term ecosystem loyalty.

---

## Tradeoffs

The strategy intentionally avoids becoming:
- A full social network.
- A luxury fashion platform.
- A broad expansion into every category.
- A generic content-heavy app. 

Instead, the focus stays on being the strongest Gen Z fashion and discovery destination, where conversion and trust matter more than unnecessary app bloat. 

---

## Why It Matters

This case study positions Flipkart to capture Gen Z more effectively by matching how they shop, what they trust, and what they care about. It combines creator-led commerce, visual AI, and circular fashion into a strategy built for the future of Indian e-commerce.
